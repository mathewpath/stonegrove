Stonegrove Edge Project · Field Instrument No. 3

The DEI Research Instrument

A working analytical instrument for planning, quality and widening-participation teams: 35,000 students across seven academic years, read through five demographic lenses — from first login to first salary.

How to read this instrument. Every cell below is a gap — the distance between the best- and worst-served group under each lens, for each stage of the student lifecycle. Warm cells are where the institution's attention should go first. Click any row to open it in the Gap Explorer.
Gap Matrix — lifecycle stage × lens
Cell = max − min group value. Colour scaled per column.
Three readings the matrix surfaces
1 · Equal access, unequal quality. Employment within 15 months barely moves across any lens (≈2 pts), but professional-level employment spreads by up to 12.7 pts and salary band by nearly 2 full bands — the gap is in which jobs, not whether jobs.
2 · Socio-economic band is the master lens. Low-SEC students fail years at 6× the rate of high-SEC students (25.0% vs 4.2%) and reach good honours at a third of the rate (21.9% vs 68.0%) — the widest spread on almost every row.
3 · Belonging is not the problem. The groups with the worst outcomes report belonging as high as — sometimes higher than — everyone else. Whatever drives these gaps, it is not social isolation; look instead at self-efficacy, attendance and the resources behind them.

Why intersect: single-lens averages hide compounding. A disclosed disability costs ~27 pts of good honours on average — but cross it with socio-economic band and the same disability costs far more at the bottom of the SEC scale than at the top. Compound disadvantage is where targeted support budgets do the most work.

% of student-years falling in each 5-mark band. Dashed line = 40 (pass) and 60 (2:1 threshold).
Reading: gaps between groups are rarely about the top of the distribution — they're about how much of each group's curve sits below the pass and 2:1 thresholds. Averages compress this; distributions show it.
Instrument notes — Stonegrove is a synthetic institutional dataset (35,001 students, AY1046-47 to 1052-53, 55 programmes across 5 faculties) built for the Edge Project; ancestry (Dwarf/Elf) and clan act as anonymised demographic proxies alongside real-structure fields for gender, disability disclosure, first-in-family status and an 8-point socio-economic rank. All figures are computed from the enrolment survey, NSS responses, ~2.2m weekly VLE/attendance records, progression outcomes, good-honours classifications and graduate outcomes, joined on student ID. Subgroup cells under n=30 are suppressed throughout. Built to be read lens-by-lens: the point is where the needle moves, not who "wins."
